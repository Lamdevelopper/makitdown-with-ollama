I believe we are more than just us; we are always part of those who came before us and eventually part of those whom we exercise influence in. I grew up in a middle-class working family; I have had the privilege to attend a private school and live with certain material commodities.

My maternal grandfather to come to Mexico from Michoacan at 18yo, to work even though he always wanted to study, he worked hard 40+years at a factory, he was so smart that he ended up doing engineer work without a formal tittle. My paternal grandfather used to sell candies on the streets of Tabasco since he was 4yo, he ended up as the top national seller of Bonos del Ahorro. Their work path the way for me to walk now.

I know that my path had been paved in a way by those who walked before me, I know that they many times, walked with no path before them, so I owe them and honor them by making the most of my life opportunities, and also know that I need eventually to give back, to close the circle by giving back, to help others, even if is just one person, one organization, one team, to also have a path, an open door, that change their life.

Being part of a team from a public University, sharing the passion for science while watching how hard is for many of them to support themselves and the team projects, made me realize there is so much to do in my Country, to help those student organizations and people who work for love of science, and someone has to do it. I want to be able to one day do it on a real deep way.

While I doubted to share the next revelation, I think is important to talk about it, because is also what make who I am. My father is alcoholic, I think, in a way, that “disadvantage” made me as I am. The second time he lost his job because of it, I realize my future was on me, ignite a flame of knowing that I need to improve myself, to grab every opportunity and give my best, and eventually be on my way, be responsible of my future. I am also grateful because despite it, my father always has giving me economical chances that many don’t have.

I have now, this enormous chance to be knocking on the door of the big leagues Universities in the world, and I know in a way, those who came before me are doing it with me, in me. I will do my best no matter what obstacles came in my way as my grandparents did . I am proud of who I am, thanks to their values, their work, their example and someday maybe someone or many will be proud of me as well.

I know where I come from in order to know where I am going

{Thank you for reading me.}

Matias Lamoyi Díaz
